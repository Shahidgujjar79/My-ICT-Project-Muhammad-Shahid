Pakistan Association for Wildlife

Founded in January 2010, the Pakistan Association for Wildlife is a non-profit conservation organization.

The foundation was incorporated with the Securities and Exchange Commission of Pakistan (SECP) in October 2010 under Corporate Universal Identification No. 0073723. It is governed by a Board of Directors, with a Technical Advisory Committee that provides support on conservation issues and trends.

Vision

An enlightened and concerned nation responsible to Mother Nature.

Mission

To enlighten the common public in Pakistan about biodiversity, environment, and social issues, aiming to foster a responsible attitude toward nature.

Objectives

  • To conserve, protect, preserve, and manage wildlife resources and their habitats.
  • To provide a platform for the public and professionals to share wildlife-related experiences and make them accessible to students, researchers, and policymakers.
  • To support hunting through sustainable wildlife development programs.

Technical Advisory Council

The Technical Advisory Council (TAC) comprises specialists from academia, wildlife departments, conservationists, and environmental activists. They provide technical assistance, advocacy, and guidance on wildlife management and conservation strategies.

Our Activities

Pakistan Wildlife Foundation is involved in various initiatives aimed at conservation, awareness, education, and research. Some of the key activities include:

  • Wildlife of Pakistan Magazine: A quarterly publication focused on wildlife-related news, issues, and research.
  • Pakistan Journal of Wildlife: A peer-reviewed journal providing a platform for young scientists to publish research without facing publication fees.
  • Wildlife Talk: A series of informal gatherings where wildlife enthusiasts and experts come together to discuss conservation strategies.
  • Wildlife Safari: Organized trips to explore Pakistan’s diverse ecosystems, flora, and fauna.
  • Wildlife Photography Field Training: Training programs for nature photographers to improve their skills in wildlife documentation.
  • Taxidermy Training: Workshops on the art of preserving animal specimens for study and display.
  • Eco-Internship Program: Offering school students hands-on experience in biodiversity conservation.
  • Distance Learning Courses: A wide range of wildlife conservation courses available online, covering topics from animal rights to wildlife photography.
